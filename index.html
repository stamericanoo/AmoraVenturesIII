1. Sulfur Burning Furnace

Process Overview

Molten sulfur is combusted with dry air to produce sulfur dioxide gas. The reaction is highly exothermic and forms the primary feed for the converter stage.

Main Reaction

S (l) + O₂ (g) → SO₂ (g)

Thermodynamic Explanation

Standard enthalpy change: -296 kJ/mol. Heat generated is recovered using waste heat boiler to produce steam for internal utility usage.

Operating Conditions

ParameterValue
Temperature900–1100°C
Pressure~1 atm
Air Excess10%
Conversion>99%

Side Reactions

ReactionImpact
2S + 3O₂ → 2SO₃Minor formation at extreme temperature
SO₃ + H₂O → H₂SO₄Acid mist formation

Design Consideration

Refractory lining required for high temperature resistance. Sulfur purity must exceed 99.5% to minimize ash formation.

Improper moisture control may result in downstream catalyst poisoning.

2. Gas Cleaning & Drying

The SO₂ gas is cooled and passed through electrostatic precipitator to remove particulates before entering drying tower.

3. Catalytic Converter

Main Reaction

2SO₂ + O₂ ⇌ 2SO₃

Operating Conditions

Temperature420–450°C
Pressure1–2 atm
CatalystV₂O₅
Catalyst Inventory120 ton

Equilibrium Consideration

Low temperature favors equilibrium conversion, but too low reduces reaction rate. Multi-bed converter balances kinetic and thermodynamic effects.

Catalyst Replacement

Catalyst replaced every 3–4 years. Annualized cost: Rp 25.7 Billion/year.

4. Absorption Tower

Sulfur trioxide is absorbed in concentrated sulfuric acid. Direct water absorption is avoided.

Temperature60–80°C
PressureAtmospheric
ReactionSO₃ + H₂SO₄ → H₂S₂O₇
Incomplete absorption may cause SO₃ slip to atmosphere.

5. Oleum Dilution

Oleum diluted carefully under temperature control.

H₂S₂O₇ + H₂O → 2H₂SO₄

Cooling jackets prevent overheating and acid mist formation.
